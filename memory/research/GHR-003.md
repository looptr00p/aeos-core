# Governance Health Report

**Traceability ID**: GHR-003

## Title

AEOS Governance Health Report — Operational Cycle 001

## Date

2026-05-18

## Governance Sustainability

**Assessment**: Sustainable at current scale, with specific warning indicators.

AEOS governance remains sustainable under Operational Cycle 001 pressure. Governance invariants (human approval, audit requirements, review requirements, escalation lifecycle) remain intact and unweakened. However, governance documentation overhead becomes repetitive at 3+ concurrent tasks under a single objective.

## Escalation Accumulation Risk

**Assessment**: Low-to-moderate risk. ESC-002 is the first accumulation indicator.

ESC-002 (review overhead) is intentionally OPEN and represents the first escalation accumulation. If future operational cycles generate similar escalations without resolution, escalation backlog becomes a governance health concern. Current recommendation: monitor escalation count per quarter. If escalations exceed 3 per quarter without resolution, governance review cadence should increase.

## Review Backlog Risk

**Assessment**: Moderate risk at 2+ concurrent reviews per objective.

REV-004 and REV-005 are both APPROVED WITH OBSERVATIONS, indicating reviews completed but with findings requiring attention. If findings accumulate without action, review backlog becomes a governance health concern. Current recommendation: track review findings across operational cycles. If unresolved findings exceed 5 per objective, prioritize finding resolution before new task creation.

## Lifecycle Maintenance Burden

**Assessment**: Manageable at current scale, concerning at 2x scale.

Active lifecycle artifacts (IN_PROGRESS tasks, ACTIVE incidents, OPEN escalations) require ongoing maintenance. At 8 active artifacts, maintenance burden is manageable. At 15+ active artifacts, lifecycle maintenance becomes a significant cognitive burden. The burden is not in the artifacts themselves but in maintaining accurate cross-references and state consistency.

## Cross-Project Coordination Risk

**Assessment**: Risk emerges at 2+ concurrent objectives.

Operational Cycle 001 operates under a single objective (OBJ-004). If a second objective becomes active concurrently, cross-project coordination risk increases significantly. Traceability references would span objectives, review scopes would overlap, and escalation priority would become ambiguous. Current recommendation: limit to 1 active objective at a time until cross-project coordination guidance is established.

## Human Review Scalability

**Assessment**: Scalable to 2-3 concurrent reviews per reviewer, then degrades.

Human review quality degrades when reviewers must context-switch between multiple concurrent reviews. At 2 reviews (REV-004, REV-005), quality remains high. At 4+ concurrent reviews, review quality likely degrades due to context-switching overhead. Current recommendation: limit reviewers to 2-3 concurrent reviews. If more reviews are needed, stagger review timing rather than running concurrently.

## Operational Warnings

1. **Review fatigue emerging** — governance requirements sections become repetitive at 3+ tasks. Monitor and consider shared reference pattern.
2. **Traceability drift risk** — manual index maintenance is error-prone under time pressure. Run aeos_lint.py consistently.
3. **Escalation accumulation** — ESC-002 is first accumulation indicator. Monitor quarterly escalation count.
4. **Coordination overhead** — grows non-linearly with active artifact count. Limit to 12 active artifacts per objective.

## Simplification Opportunities

1. **Shared governance requirements reference** — tasks under the same objective could reference a shared governance block instead of duplicating.
2. **Review consolidation guidance** — for objectives with 4+ concurrent tasks, consolidate review types more aggressively.
3. **Quick-resume handoff summaries** — add executive summary section to handoffs with 3+ active artifacts.
4. **Traceability update checklist** — add explicit checklist to task templates reminding authors to update TRACEABILITY_INDEX.md.

## Scaling Concerns

1. **Active artifact count** — AEOS scales well to ~12 active artifacts per objective. Beyond this, coordination overhead becomes the primary constraint.
2. **Concurrent objectives** — AEOS has not been validated under 2+ concurrent objectives. This is the next scaling frontier.
3. **Review reviewer capacity** — Human reviewers can handle 2-3 concurrent reviews effectively. Beyond this, review quality degrades.
4. **Traceability index size** — TRACEABILITY_INDEX.md is currently manageable at 27+ artifacts. At 50+ artifacts, navigation becomes difficult.

## Conclusion

AEOS governance is healthy and sustainable under Operational Cycle 001 conditions. Governance invariants remain intact. The primary health concerns are operational friction points (review fatigue, traceability maintenance, coordination overhead) rather than governance failures. These should be addressed through guidance refinement and operational discipline, not structural governance changes.
