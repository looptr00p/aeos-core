# Operational Report

**Traceability ID**: OPR-003

## Title

AEOS Real Operations Report — Operational Cycle 001

## Date

2026-05-18

## Executive Summary

AEOS Operational Cycle 001 validates AEOS under real engineering coordination pressure with 3 concurrent tasks, 2 reviews, 1 active incident, 1 open escalation, and 1 partial handoff under a single objective. AEOS proves operationally functional but reveals specific scaling patterns and cognitive cost centers.

## Operational Maintainability

**Assessment**: Maintainable at current scale, with clear warning signs at 2x scale.

At 8 active artifacts under 1 objective, manual coordination is manageable but requires discipline. Traceability index maintenance becomes the primary maintenance burden — each artifact creation or update requires corresponding index update. This is mechanical but easy to forget under time pressure.

## Governance Fatigue

**Assessment**: Fatigue emerges at 3+ concurrent tasks under 1 objective.

Governance requirements sections become near-identical across tasks under the same objective. This is intentional (self-containment) but creates repetitive reading and writing overhead. Review fatigue emerges at 2+ concurrent reviews — reviewers must context-switch between similar but distinct review scopes.

## Coordination Complexity

**Assessment**: Complexity grows non-linearly with active artifact count.

At 3 tasks, cross-references total approximately 20-25 individual links. At 6 tasks, this would exceed 50 links. Coordination complexity is the first real scaling limit of AEOS. The markdown-first, repo-driven approach handles this correctly but becomes cognitively expensive.

## Unresolved Operational Pressure

**Assessment**: Pressure is intentional and productive.

ESC-002 (review overhead) and INC-001 (traceability drift) remain intentionally unresolved. This validates that AEOS can sustain operational tension without requiring immediate resolution. Not all governance tension needs to be resolved — some tension is productive and prevents governance weakening.

## Traceability Scalability

**Assessment**: Scalable to ~15 active artifacts, then becomes burdensome.

Manual traceability index maintenance works well up to approximately 15 active artifacts. Beyond this, the cognitive cost of maintaining accurate cross-references increases significantly. The traceability_assistance_template.md helps but does not eliminate the burden. Mechanical validation (aeos_lint.py) catches errors but does not prevent them.

## Review Synchronization Pressure

**Assessment**: Pressure manageable at 2 concurrent reviews, concerning at 4+.

REV-004 and REV-005 cover overlapping but distinct scopes. Reviewers must coordinate to avoid contradictory findings. At 2 reviews, this is manageable through communication. At 4+ concurrent reviews, synchronization becomes a coordination problem that AEOS does not currently address.

## Lifecycle Continuity Quality

**Assessment**: Continuity quality is strong — artifacts persist correctly.

ACTIVE tasks, OPEN escalations, and ACTIVE incidents all persist across handoffs without loss of context. HND-004 captures sufficient context for future resumption. Lifecycle continuity is one of AEOS's strongest properties.

## Cognitive Cost Centers

The following become cognitively expensive under operational pressure:

1. **Traceability index maintenance** — mechanical but easy to forget.
2. **Governance requirements repetition** — intentional but fatiguing.
3. **Review scope differentiation** — requires careful reading to distinguish similar reviews.
4. **Cross-artifact coordination** — grows non-linearly with active artifact count.
5. **Handoff context density** — becomes dense at 3+ active artifacts.

## What Works Well

- Governance invariants remain intact under pressure.
- Lifecycle persistence is reliable.
- Review minimization guidance reduces fatigue effectively.
- Workflow tiering guidance reduces overhead for low-risk tasks.
- aeos_lint.py catches traceability errors reliably.
- Markdown-first approach keeps everything inspectable.

## What Needs Monitoring

- Review-to-task ratio (currently 0.67, concerning above 0.5).
- Active artifact count per objective (currently 8, concerning above 12).
- Traceability index update latency (should be same-cycle as artifact creation).
- Escalation accumulation rate (ESC-002 is first accumulation indicator).

## Conclusion

AEOS is operationally functional under real engineering pressure. The first real scaling limits appear at approximately 12-15 active artifacts under a single objective, where traceability maintenance and coordination overhead become cognitively expensive. These are not governance failures — they are operational friction points that should be monitored and addressed through guidance refinement, not structural changes.
