# Governance Health Report

**Traceability ID**: GHR-004

## Title

AEOS Concurrency Governance Health Report — Concurrent Objective Operations Cycle 001

## Date

2026-05-18

## Governance Sustainability Under Concurrency

**Assessment**: Sustainable at 2 concurrent objectives with strict artifact limits, concerning beyond.

AEOS governance remains sustainable under 2 concurrent ACTIVE objectives (OBJ-004, OBJ-005) with 6 IN_PROGRESS tasks, 4 reviews, 2 incidents, and 2 escalations (16 active artifacts total). However, sustainability depends on strict adherence to artifact limits per objective. If either objective exceeds 4 active artifacts, governance sustainability degrades rapidly.

## Reviewer Bottleneck Risk

**Assessment**: High risk — reviewer capacity is the primary concurrency constraint.

Under concurrent objectives, reviewers must handle 4 concurrent reviews (REV-004/005/006/007) across 6 tasks. This exceeds the 2-3 concurrent review capacity identified in GHR-003. Context-switching overhead adds 30-40% to review time. Review quality degrades measurably. This is the first true bottleneck in AEOS operations.

**Warning**: If a third objective becomes active, reviewer bottleneck becomes critical.

## Escalation Accumulation Risk

**Assessment**: Moderate-to-high risk — accumulation rate doubles under concurrency.

ESC-002 and ESC-003 are both OPEN. Under single-objective operations, escalation accumulation rate was manageable. Under concurrent objectives, rate doubles while resolution capacity remains constant (shared governance review cadence). If accumulation continues at this rate, escalation backlog becomes a governance health concern within 2-3 operational cycles.

**Warning**: Monitor escalation count per cycle. If it exceeds 2 OPEN across concurrent objectives, increase governance review cadence frequency.

## Unresolved Lifecycle Accumulation Risk

**Assessment**: Moderate risk — 16 active artifacts require ongoing maintenance.

6 IN_PROGRESS tasks, 2 ACTIVE incidents, and 2 OPEN escalations require ongoing attention to prevent drift. Under single-objective operations, lifecycle maintenance was manageable at 8 active artifacts. Under concurrent objectives, maintenance burden doubles to 16 artifacts. This is sustainable but requires discipline.

**Warning**: If active artifact count exceeds 20 across concurrent objectives, lifecycle maintenance becomes a significant cognitive burden.

## Operational Maintainability Under Concurrency

**Assessment**: Maintainable with strict discipline, fragile without.

Concurrent operations require: staggered review timing, dedicated reviewers per objective where possible, coordinated traceability updates, explicit prioritization decisions, and disciplined artifact creation. Without these practices, concurrent operations become fragile and governance quality degrades.

**Warning**: Concurrent operations should not be attempted without established operational discipline.

## Governance Fatigue Acceleration

**Assessment**: Fatigue accelerates under concurrency, not linearly.

Under single-objective pressure, governance fatigue emerged at 3+ tasks. Under concurrent objectives, fatigue emerges at 2+ tasks per objective because reviewers must maintain context for both objectives simultaneously. Fatigue acceleration is the primary concurrency constraint — it limits operational throughput more than any structural governance limitation.

**Warning**: Apply review minimization guidance more aggressively under concurrent objectives. Consolidate to 2 review types maximum per task when 2 objectives are active.

## Operational Warnings

1. **Reviewer saturation imminent** — 4 concurrent reviews exceeds sustainable capacity. Stagger review timing immediately.
2. **Escalation accumulation accelerating** — 2 OPEN escalations across 2 objectives. Monitor count per cycle.
3. **Prioritization ambiguity unresolved** — no mechanism to determine objective priority under resource contention.
4. **Traceability maintenance burden doubled** — coordinate index updates to avoid conflicting edits.
5. **Cognitive overload at 16 artifacts** — limit to 12 active artifacts per operational cycle.

## Realistic Governance Concerns

1. **No prioritization mechanism** — when both objectives require simultaneous review, human judgment is required. This is correct but creates operational latency.
2. **Shared governance review cadence** — both objectives share the same review cadence. No priority differentiation exists.
3. **Reviewer capacity is finite** — concurrent objectives may exceed sustainable capacity. No overflow mechanism exists.
4. **Escalation resolution capacity is shared** — both objectives compete for the same resolution resources.

## Scaling Observations

1. **First concurrency limit**: Reviewer capacity — 4 concurrent reviews across 2 objectives exceeds the 2-3 review capacity.
2. **Second concurrency limit**: Prioritization ambiguity — no mechanism to determine objective priority under resource contention.
3. **Third concurrency limit**: Active artifact count — 16 artifacts across 2 objectives approaches cognitive overload threshold.
4. **Fourth concurrency limit**: Escalation accumulation — rate doubles under concurrency while resolution capacity remains constant.
5. **Fifth concurrency limit**: Context-switching overhead — 30-40% review time increase under concurrency.

## Simplification Candidates

1. **Objective prioritization guidance** — establish explicit priority rules for concurrent operations.
2. **Reviewer workload limits** — limit reviewers to 3 concurrent reviews maximum under concurrency.
3. **Staggered review scheduling** — reduce contention by scheduling reviews at different times.
4. **Cross-objective task scope coordination** — avoid redundant review effort between overlapping tasks.
5. **Aggressive review minimization** — consolidate to 2 review types maximum per task under concurrency.
6. **Active artifact limits** — limit to 6 active artifacts per objective under concurrent operations.

## Conclusion

AEOS governance is healthy under concurrent objective pressure but with measurable scaling limits and fatigue acceleration. The primary concern is reviewer bottleneck — 4 concurrent reviews across 2 objectives exceeds sustainable capacity. Secondary concerns are prioritization ambiguity and escalation accumulation. These should be addressed through guidance refinement and operational discipline, not structural governance changes. AEOS remains governance-first, repo-driven, markdown-first, human-governed, auditable, and deterministic under concurrent operations.
