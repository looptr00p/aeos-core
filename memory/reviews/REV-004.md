## Description

Review of governance workflow applicability and operational sustainability under active multi-task coordination pressure (TASK-004, TASK-005, TASK-006).

## Scope

- Validate workflow tiering guidance compliance across TASK-004/005/006.
- Validate review minimization strategy compliance.
- Assess governance fatigue risk under concurrent task pressure.
- Identify simplification opportunities in governance layering.

## Findings

### Finding 1: Governance Layering Becomes Repetitive at 3+ Concurrent Tasks

When TASK-004, TASK-005, and TASK-006 all reference the same governance documents (GOVERNANCE_SEVERITY_MODEL.md, REVIEW_REQUIREMENTS.md, WORKFLOW_TIERING_GUIDANCE.md), the governance requirements section becomes near-identical across all three tasks. This creates cognitive overhead without adding governance value.

**Severity**: LOW
**Recommendation**: Consider a shared governance requirements reference pattern for tasks under the same objective.

### Finding 2: Review Minimization Works but Requires Discipline

TASK-004 consolidates to 3 review types, TASK-005 to 2, TASK-006 to 3. This is effective but requires the task author to consciously apply minimization guidance. Without discipline, review creep returns quickly.

**Severity**: LOW
**Recommendation**: No structural change needed. Guidance document is sufficient.

### Finding 3: Workflow Stages Remain Practical Under Pressure

All 12 workflow stages remain applicable. Tiering guidance correctly reduces stages for MEDIUM-tier tasks without weakening governance. No workflow stage was found to be obsolete or redundant.

**Severity**: N/A — positive finding.

### Finding 4: Review Fatigue Risk Identified

With 2 reviews (REV-004, REV-005) covering 3 tasks under 1 objective, plus 1 incident and 1 escalation, the review-to-task ratio is 0.67. This is sustainable for 3 tasks but would become fatiguing at 6+ concurrent tasks under a single objective.

**Severity**: MEDIUM
**Recommendation**: Monitor review-to-task ratio. If it exceeds 0.5 under a single objective, consider review consolidation guidance.

## Decision

APPROVED WITH OBSERVATIONS

Governance workflows remain operationally sustainable. Observations document emerging fatigue patterns that should be monitored but do not require immediate structural changes.
