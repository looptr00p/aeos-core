## Findings

### Finding 1: Delayed Review Timing Causes Continuity Degradation

INC-003 (delayed governance review cycle) demonstrates that review timing delays cause operational context to become stale. Tasks created in Cycle 004 (TASK-004/005/006) may have stale context by Cycle 006. Cross-objective coordination overhead grows as unresolved issues accumulate. Continuity degradation is gradual but measurable — lifecycle state tracking becomes less reliable over time.

**Severity**: MEDIUM
**Recommendation**: Re-establish governance review cadence immediately. Update operational context for stale artifacts before review resumption.

### Finding 2: Operational Debt Accumulation Exceeds Resolution Capacity

6 IN_PROGRESS tasks, 3 ACTIVE incidents, 1 IN_REVIEW escalation, and 1 OPEN escalation represent accumulated operational debt. Resolution capacity (shared governance review cadence) cannot keep pace with accumulation rate. Net debt grows with each operational cycle. This is the governance debt trap — debt accumulates faster than it can be resolved.

**Severity**: MEDIUM
**Recommendation**: Prioritize debt resolution over new task creation. Limit new artifacts until existing debt is reduced.

### Finding 3: Governance Sustainability Under Prolonged Pressure

AEOS governance remains functional under prolonged pressure but with measurable degradation: review quality decreases, continuity reliability declines, operational debt accumulates, and reviewer fatigue persists. Governance invariants remain intact — no weakening has occurred. However, sustainability is questionable if pressure continues without resolution.

**Severity**: MEDIUM
**Recommendation**: Establish maximum sustained pressure duration — if unresolved pressure exceeds 3 operational cycles, mandatory governance review must occur.

### Finding 4: Stabilization Attempts Are Partially Effective

ESC-002 partial recovery (IN_REVIEW) and staggered review timing demonstrate that stabilization attempts have measurable but limited effectiveness. Recovery capacity grows slower than governance pressure accumulation — this is the fundamental stabilization constraint. Stabilization can slow debt accumulation but cannot reverse it without resolving underlying pressure sources.

**Severity**: LOW
**Recommendation**: Continue stabilization attempts but set realistic expectations — stabilization slows degradation but does not eliminate it.

## Decision

APPROVED WITH OBSERVATIONS

Delayed continuity assessment reveals measurable degradation under prolonged pressure but no governance collapse. Observations document that stabilization is partially effective but insufficient to reverse debt accumulation.
