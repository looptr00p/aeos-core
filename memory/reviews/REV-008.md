## Description

Review of governance recovery coordination and reviewer recovery capacity under sustained OBJ-004 and OBJ-005 concurrent operations pressure.

- Assess reviewer recovery capacity under sustained load.
- Evaluate staggered review timing effectiveness.
- Identify governance recovery limitations.
- Document reviewer fatigue persistence.
- Assess incomplete mitigation effectiveness.

## Findings

### Finding 1: Recovery Capacity Grows Slower Than Governance Pressure Accumulation

Under sustained concurrent operations, governance pressure accumulates faster than recovery capacity can address it. ESC-002 moved to IN_REVIEW (partial recovery) but ESC-003 remains OPEN (sustained pressure). INC-003 emerged as a new incident (delayed review cycle) while INC-001 and INC-002 remain ACTIVE. The net effect is that governance debt continues to grow despite recovery attempts. Recovery capacity grows slower than governance pressure accumulation.

**Severity**: MEDIUM
**Recommendation**: No structural change needed. This is an inherent property of sustained governance pressure — recovery is inherently slower than accumulation because recovery requires resolution while accumulation only requires new issues.

### Finding 2: Reviewer Fatigue Persists Despite Mitigation

Staggered review timing reduced simultaneous demand by ~25% but did not eliminate reviewer fatigue. Context-switching overhead persists at 30-40%. Reviewer-agent workload remains at saturation. Recovery attempts (TASK-010) are insufficient to clear backlog while new review demand (REV-008, REV-009) continues to arrive.

**Severity**: MEDIUM
**Recommendation**: Limit new review creation until existing backlog is cleared. This requires operational discipline, not structural change.

### Finding 3: Incomplete Mitigation Effectiveness

ESC-002 partial recovery (IN_REVIEW) demonstrates that mitigation actions can reduce pressure but not resolve it. Review minimization guidance applied more aggressively but fatigue persists. Staggered review timing helps but does not eliminate contention. The gap between mitigation effectiveness and full resolution is the governance debt accumulation zone.

**Severity**: LOW
**Recommendation**: Document mitigation effectiveness honestly — partial recovery is progress but not resolution.

### Finding 4: Governance Recovery Limitations

Recovery is limited by: reviewer capacity (finite), governance review cadence (shared), escalation resolution authority (human judgment only), and operational prioritization (no explicit mechanism). These limitations are inherent to AEOS governance model and cannot be resolved without weakening governance invariants.

**Severity**: MEDIUM
**Recommendation**: Accept recovery limitations as inherent to governance-first design. Focus on managing debt accumulation rate rather than eliminating debt entirely.

## Decision

APPROVED WITH OBSERVATIONS

Governance recovery coordination is functional but limited by inherent capacity constraints. Observations document that recovery capacity grows slower than governance pressure accumulation — this is the primary stabilization concern.
