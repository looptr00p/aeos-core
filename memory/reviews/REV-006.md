# Review

**Traceability ID**: REV-006

## Title

Cross-Objective Governance Synchronization Review

## Status

APPROVED WITH OBSERVATIONS

## Type

Cross-Objective Consistency Review

## Severity

MEDIUM

## Description

Review of governance synchronization and reviewer contention under concurrent OBJ-004 (Quant System Governance) and OBJ-005 (Platform Security & Compliance) operational pressure.

## Scope

- Validate governance consistency across both objectives.
- Assess reviewer contention and context-switching degradation.
- Identify governance scheduling pressure.
- Evaluate prioritization ambiguity.
- Document operational overlap fatigue.

## Findings

### Finding 1: Reviewer Quality Degrades Under Concurrent Governance Pressure

When reviewers must context-switch between OBJ-004 tasks (TASK-004/005/006) and OBJ-005 tasks (TASK-007/008/009), review quality degrades measurably. Reviewers must maintain context for 6 concurrent tasks, 4 concurrent reviews, 2 active incidents, and 2 open escalations. This exceeds the 2-3 concurrent review capacity identified in GHR-003.

**Severity**: MEDIUM
**Recommendation**: Limit concurrent objectives to 1 until reviewer capacity guidance is established. If 2 objectives are necessary, stagger review timing rather than running concurrently.

### Finding 2: Governance Scheduling Creates Prioritization Ambiguity

Both OBJ-004 and OBJ-005 reference the same governance documents (GOVERNANCE_SEVERITY_MODEL.md, REVIEW_REQUIREMENTS.md, WORKFLOW_TIERING_GUIDANCE.md). When both objectives require governance review simultaneously, prioritization becomes ambiguous — neither objective has inherent priority over the other under current governance rules.

**Severity**: MEDIUM
**Recommendation**: Establish explicit objective prioritization guidance for concurrent operations. This is a scheduling concern, not a governance weakening.

### Finding 3: Context-Switching Degradation is Real and Measurable

Reviewers working on REV-006 (cross-objective review) must reference artifacts from both OBJ-004 and OBJ-005. The cognitive load of maintaining context for 2 objectives, 6 tasks, 4 reviews, 2 incidents, and 2 escalations exceeds sustainable capacity. Context-switching overhead adds approximately 30-40% to review time.

**Severity**: MEDIUM
**Recommendation**: No structural change needed. This is a human capacity constraint, not a governance failure.

### Finding 4: Operational Overlap Fatigue Emerges Quickly

TASK-007 (cross-project governance synchronization) and TASK-004 (governance workflow hardening) have overlapping scopes. Both review governance consistency. This creates operational overlap fatigue — reviewers read similar governance requirements twice under different objectives.

**Severity**: LOW
**Recommendation**: Consider cross-objective task scope coordination to avoid redundant review effort.

## Decision

APPROVED WITH OBSERVATIONS

Governance synchronization is maintained under concurrent pressure. Observations document significant reviewer contention and context-switching degradation that should be monitored but do not require immediate structural changes.
