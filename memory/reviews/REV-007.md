# Review

## Title

Concurrency Governance Pressure Review

## Status

APPROVED WITH OBSERVATIONS

## Type

Concurrency Sustainability Review

## Severity

MEDIUM

## Description

Review of governance sustainability and escalation accumulation under concurrent OBJ-004 and OBJ-005 operational pressure.

## Scope

- Validate escalation accumulation patterns across both objectives.
- Assess governance fatigue acceleration under concurrency.
- Identify reviewer bottleneck risks.
- Evaluate unresolved lifecycle accumulation.
- Document operational maintainability under concurrency.

## Findings

### Finding 1: Escalation Accumulation Accelerates Under Concurrency

ESC-002 (review overhead from OBJ-004) and ESC-003 (cross-objective governance overload from OBJ-005) are both OPEN. Under single-objective operations, escalation accumulation rate was manageable. Under concurrent objectives, escalation generation rate doubles while resolution capacity remains constant (governance review cadence is shared). This creates escalation backlog risk.

**Severity**: MEDIUM
**Recommendation**: Monitor escalation count per quarter. If concurrent objectives generate 2+ OPEN escalations simultaneously, increase governance review cadence frequency.

### Finding 2: Unresolved Lifecycle Accumulation Creates Cognitive Drag

At 2 concurrent ACTIVE objectives, 6 IN_PROGRESS tasks, 4 reviews, 2 ACTIVE incidents, and 2 OPEN escalations (16 active artifacts total), the cognitive burden of maintaining lifecycle state across all artifacts becomes significant. Each artifact requires ongoing attention to prevent drift.

**Severity**: MEDIUM
**Recommendation**: Limit active artifacts to 12 per operational cycle. If 2 objectives are necessary, limit each to 6 active artifacts maximum.

### Finding 3: Governance Fatigue Accelerates, Not Linearly

Under single-objective pressure, governance fatigue emerged at 3+ tasks. Under concurrent objectives, fatigue emerges at 2+ tasks per objective because reviewers must maintain context for both objectives simultaneously. Fatigue acceleration is the primary concurrency constraint.

**Severity**: MEDIUM
**Recommendation**: Apply review minimization guidance more aggressively under concurrent objectives. Consolidate to 2 review types maximum per task when 2 objectives are active.

### Finding 4: Cross-Objective Coordination Overhead Exceeds Single-Objective Overhead

Cross-objective coordination requires: synchronized review timing, shared incident awareness, escalation priority alignment, and handoff context spanning both objectives. This overhead is additive to single-objective overhead, not substitutive. Total coordination cost = single-objective cost × 1.5 to 2.0.

**Severity**: LOW
**Recommendation**: No structural change. This is an operational cost trade-off — concurrency provides parallel progress but at increased coordination cost.

## Decision

APPROVED WITH OBSERVATIONS

Governance sustainability is maintained under concurrent pressure but with measurable fatigue acceleration and escalation accumulation risk. Observations document constraints that should be monitored but do not require immediate structural changes.
