# Objective

**Traceability ID**: OBJ-008

## Title

Governance Pressure Simulation Framework — Observability and Longitudinal Measurement

## Status

DRAFT

## Description

Design a lightweight operational framework for observing, measuring, and understanding governance behavior under sustained operational pressure. This objective creates metrics, simulation scenarios, a markdown-first dashboard, and an operational observation report — enabling longitudinal study of governance dynamics without introducing automation, orchestration, or hidden state.

## Strategic Purpose

AEOS Core has comprehensive governance documentation and an enforcement layer design (OBJ-007). However, AEOS lacks the ability to observe how governance behaves under real operational pressure. We know from operational experience (OBJ-004, OBJ-005) that reviewer contention, escalation accumulation, and recovery asymmetry are real phenomena. But we cannot measure them systematically, predict their behavior, or study their longitudinal patterns. This objective establishes the observability layer that makes governance behavior measurable, trackable, and researchable.

## Problem Statement

AEOS governance operates under organizational dynamics that are currently unmeasured:

1. **Governance pressure accumulates**: Each active objective, open incident, and unresolved escalation adds pressure to the governance system. We observe this qualitatively but cannot measure it quantitatively.
2. **Recovery is asymmetric**: Governance pressure accumulates faster than it can be resolved. ESC-002 remains IN_REVIEW while new escalations emerge. We know this is true but cannot quantify the asymmetry.
3. **Reviewer bandwidth is finite**: Under concurrent objectives (OBJ-004 + OBJ-005), reviewer-agent and auditor-agent share 6 tasks and 4 reviews. We observe contention but cannot model saturation.
4. **Governance debt persists**: Resolved incidents leave residual organizational effects. INC-001 is RESOLVED but its effects persist in TASK-004 through TASK-007. We cannot measure debt accumulation or decay.
5. **No longitudinal data**: All observations are point-in-time. We cannot track governance trends, detect drift, or study recovery patterns over time.

Without systematic observability, governance improvements are based on intuition rather than measurement.

## Desired Outcome

A markdown-first governance observability framework that enables measurement of governance pressure, simulation of operational stress scenarios, longitudinal tracking of governance health, and research into organizational dynamics — all without introducing automation, databases, or runtime complexity.

## Scope

### Included

- Define 12 governance metrics with formulas and thresholds
- Design 8 operational pressure simulation scenarios
- Create markdown-first governance dashboard template
- Create first operational observation report (OPR-007)
- Create ADR-003: "Operational Governance Observability"
- Define observation cadence and measurement strategy
- Design longitudinal research framework
- Define operational entropy indicators
- Design governance debt tracking methodology

### Excluded

- No automated metric collection
- No databases or data stores
- No visualization engines or dashboards (beyond markdown)
- No real-time monitoring
- No AI-driven analysis or prediction
- No autonomous alerting or notification
- No cloud infrastructure
- No runtime agents or orchestration
- No changes to existing governance rules or protocols
- No changes to enforcement layer (OBJ-007)

## Constraints

- Must remain markdown-first — all metrics, dashboards, and reports are human-readable markdown
- Must remain repository-driven — all data stored in git-versioned files
- Must remain deterministic — metric calculations produce the same result given the same input
- Must remain locally executable — no cloud dependencies
- Must remain lightweight — measurement overhead must not exceed 10% of operational effort
- Must preserve human authority — metrics inform decisions, they do not make them
- Must not introduce hidden state — all measurement data is visible in repository
- Must be maintainable by a single human operator
- Must not modify existing governance documents

## Risks

| Risk | Severity | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|------------|
| Metrics become measurement overhead | MEDIUM | Medium | Cap at 12 metrics; each must be calculable in <5 minutes |
| Simulation scenarios are unrealistic | LOW | Medium | Base scenarios on actual operational data (OBJ-004, OBJ-005) |
| Dashboard template becomes stale | LOW | Low | Dashboard is regenerated per observation cycle, not maintained continuously |
| Longitudinal analysis requires more data than available | MEDIUM | High | Start with available data; identify data gaps for future collection |
| Metrics interpreted as governance automation | LOW | Low | Explicit documentation that metrics inform, not decide |
| Operational entropy indicators are too abstract | LOW | Medium | Ground each indicator in observable repository state |
